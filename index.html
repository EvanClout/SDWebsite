Biomedical Monitoring for Astronauts
Senior Design Team 310

About The Project

Spacesuit design is currently an active area of research. Engineers and scientists are always working to improve current and future missions in space. Working in a pressurized suit thousands of miles from Earth presents a range of challenges. These include monitoring the in-suit environment and the astronaut’s health. Team 310 has created a sensor system for monitoring an astronaut’s health and environment. Our goal was to accurately measure critical metrics for research and safety purposes.

The sensor system consists of three modules, the first of which being a set of environmental sensors for measuring CO2 concentration, ambient temperature, and humidity. To monitor astronaut health, a set of biomedical sensors on the foot is used to measure heart rate, galvanic skin resistance (sweat levels) and body temperature. Finally, a main processing unit takes care of reading and interpreting sensor data. The team designed and fabricated a custom printed circuit board to house the processor. The project was a proof-of-concept design that monitors an astronaut’s vital signs and the environmental conditions in a spacesuit.

In general, the device can measure selected variables, store them locally, and transmit the data to an external device. The final deliverable from the team serves as a prototype for a sensor unit that could be used during an extravehicular activity (EVA) mission.

Project Timeline

Fall 2023 - Spring 2024

  • Beginning of Fall 2023:

    Project Assignment

    Shortly after our team was formed and our project was assigned, we were met with the unfortunate news that L3Harris would be unable to work with us due to legal concerns. Thus, our team began modifying the original project definition.

  • Further into Fall 2023:

    New Project Definition

    After some time and deliberation of whether or not we wanted to keep/modify the original project scope, or create our own project, our team decided on the latter. This is when we decided upon the idea of a space suit sensor suit. Our team began the process of defining our own problem statement, project scope, as well as our final deliverables.

  • End of Fall 2023:

    Bill of Materials

    Upon the creation of our new project idea, the preliminary detailed design was created and the component selection process was completed.

  • Beginning of Spring 2024:

    Creating initial prototype

    Once we recieved most of our parts, initial prototyping began. Shortly into the spring semester, some additional components became necessary and were added to the BOM.

  • Further into Spring 2024:

    Final Design Synthesis & Testing

    As our prototyping continued, testing eventually begun.
